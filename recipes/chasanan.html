Chasanan Makh

Description:

This is the most traditional, simplest, and ubiquitous dish of the mongolian nomads. The meat of an animal (usually mutton) is cut into handy chunks together with the bones, and boiled in salted water until ready. Originally, this was the full meal, today some vegetables or a condiment like Ketchup are usually added. The meat is eaten with the fingers, using a sharp knife

The head of the family or the guest of honor gets the first and largest portion, then the others may help themselves. A strengthening meal like this is considered mandatory especially before departing on a journey. An early morning departure turns this specific dish into a breakfast, which may take some getting used to for foreign visitors.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef or lamb (preferably with some fat), cut into small cubes
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant.
  3. Add the beef or lamb cubes to the pan. Cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the soy sauce, ground black pepper, salt, cumin, and paprika. Mix well to coat the meat with the seasonings.
  5. Add the water to the pan. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  6. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 1-2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded together. Stir occasionally, and add more water if necessary to keep the meat moist.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
  8. Enjoy your meal!
